Why This Setup

Use one interviewer, not two competing questioners. The PM lane asks; other lanes synthesize quietly.

Ask each person what success looks like, then compare. Catches mismatched expectations before building.

🌊
Interview Flow — 6 Steps
1

Lock the Frame

Confirm the single job for Alice tonight: reading help, language help, or relationship/collab help. No scope drift.

2

Shared Vision Round

Ask both stakeholders the same 4-5 questions to expose overlap and tension before they become blockers.

3

Individual Pull

Ask each person what they care about most, what they'll forgive, and what would make them say "wow."

4

Synthesis Checkpoint

Speak back the product thesis in one sentence. If both agree, freeze it. If not, iterate now.

5

Tiny Demo Selection

Choose the shortest happy path that proves the thesis: open passage → tap phrase → hear/see explanation.

6

Acceptance Criteria

CodexSpec writes three criteria. Review lane tries to break them. Tiger Mom trims until they're shippable.

❓
High-Value Interview Questions

👥 Ask Both Together

  1. When Alice is working well, what exact moment makes you smile?
  2. Who is the first user: you two, language learners, ADHD readers, or kids?
  3. What is the one job Alice must do on day one?
  4. What can be fake/stubbed in the demo, and what must feel real?
  5. If we only show one 60-second flow, what should it be?
  6. Optimize for delight, learning value, or technical depth?

🎭 Ask Each Individually

  1. What part do you most want to own?
  2. What part would frustrate you if it were missing?
  3. What is your biggest fear for this product?
  4. What feature are you tempted to add that should wait?
  5. What would make you call the first version a win tonight?
  6. What do you want the other person to hear from you?
